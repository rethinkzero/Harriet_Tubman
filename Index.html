Harriet Tubman

Harriet Tubman was born between 1820 and 1822 in Dorchester Country, Maryland, and died on March 10, 1913, in Auburn, NY. Harriet escaped slavery and discovered the underground railroad. She was the conductor of the train that helped 300 or more slaves escape from slavery. Harriet fought in the Civil War of America as a spy, guerrilla soldier, scout, and nurse. She was also part of the Civil Rights movement that stated that slavery should band from the United States.

Harriet's importance to African American culture is enormous. She is the African American woman that not only helped her fellow people. She is also the woman who fought for African Americans' rights during the Civil War. She is seen as a hero for the Afro-American community. All of her achievements and accomplishments led to her becoming a part of the African American culture. And that's why her legacy is so important to African American month.
